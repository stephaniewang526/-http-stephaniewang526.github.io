Wang vs. Stark



This video is an interesting experiment on digital technology as a medium. By changing the combinations of voice and face using Premier Pro, the clip explores how technology IS the medium in digital art. The combinations presented in this video are:
1) Wang's face with Wang's voice,
2) Mosaic mask on Stark's face with Wang's voice,
3) Stark's face with Wang's voice, and
4) Stark's face with Stark's voice.
These are only a sample of possibilities that technology is able to create. The aim is to demonstrate the immense ability that technology possesses in making and defining digital art.

  • Source: Game of Thrones, Season 1, Episode 2
  • Date: May 2016
  • Artist: Stephanie Qian Wang

Who is Geoffrey?



Who is Geoffrey? By manipulating voice and face, the character's identity and intention vary depending on the combinations of video processing techniques used. The synthetic approach upon a synthetic basis allows us to explore visual feedback and perception in another dimension.

  • Source: Game of Thrones, Season 1, Episode 2
  • Date: May 2016
  • Artist: Stephanie Qian Wang

The Hound



Unlike the first video, this video explores the effect of manipulating background on the perception of the content. The technology use not only limits what is available and what is not to the viewer, it also directly determines the kind of work that is delivered to the user. The effects presented in the video are:
1) Bacground mosaic mask of 60 by 60 pixel blocks,
2) Bacground mosaic mask of 20 by 20 pixel blocks,
3) Face and voice change with no background mosaic mask, and
4) Face and voice change with mosaic mask of 10 by 10 pixel blocks.
Viewers are expected to have very different experiences with these experimental approaches. The aim, again, is to demonstrate the immense ability that technology possesses in making and defining digital art.

  • Source: Game of Thrones, Season 4, Episode 1
  • Date: May 2016
  • Artist: Stephanie Qian Wang

Self Portrait in Pencil



This self portrait was created using a gradient of tonality from black to white following a mechanic and algorithmic method.
Each grid is 0.5' in unit. Paper is 30' by 20'.

  • Location: Sibley Hall, Cornell University
  • Date: Feburary 2016
  • Artist: Stephanie Qian Wang

Self Portrait in Objects



Building on the experience with the previous algorithm drawing work, this work uses computer grid as a structural approach to color in a physical form.
Computer is used as a drawing tool to create a sculpture based on the source.
The sculpture is made with over 200 push pins, a pair of Tommy Hilfiger sunglasses, and a foam board of dimension 11' by 14'.

  • Location: Sibley Hall, Cornell University
  • Date: Feburary 2016
  • Artist: Stephanie Qian Wang
